Jim Comey Biography

James Brien Comey was born in Yonkers, New York on December 14, 1960. He is the son of J. Brien Comey, a corporate real estate agent, and Joan Marie, a computer consultant and homemaker.

In the early 1970s, his family relocated to Allendale, New Jersey. E attended Allendale’s Northern Highlands Regional High School. He received his bachelor’s degree in religion and chemistry from the College of William and Mary in 1982.

He earned his Juris Doctor (J.D.) from the University of Chicago Law School in 1985. Comey and his brother were victims of a home invasion in 1977 by “The Ramsey Rapist,” a criminal gang. His siblings’ names are Peter, Christopher, and Patricia Comey.

Career

Comey began his legal career as a law clerk for then-United States District Judge John M. Walker Jr. He then went on to work as an associate for Gibson, Dunn & Crutcher’s New York office. He worked in the United States Attorney’s Office for the Southern District of New York from 1987 to 1993.

From 1996 to 2001, he served as the managing Assistant United States Attorney for the Eastern District of Virginia. He also served as the Senate Whitewater committee’s deputy special counsel. Comey is also a published author, with his first book, “A Higher Loyalty: Truth, Lies, and Leadership,” covering leadership, ethics, and his government experience.

In 2013, he was appointed as Director of the FBI by then-President Barack Obama. In his position, he was tasked with supervising the FBI’s investigation into the Hillary Clinton email scandal. From 2005 to 2013, Comey left the government and worked privately as a senior vice president and general counsel for Lockheed Martin, the largest contractor to the US Department of Defense. Then, in 2010, he announced his departure from Lockheed Martin to join Bridgewater Associates, a Connecticut-based investment management firm.
