Ronald Isley Biography

Ronald Isley is an American recording artist, songwriter, record producer, and occasional actor who is also known as Mr. Biggs. Isley is well known as the lead singer and founding member of the family music group the Isley Brothers.

Isley began singing at the age of two, winning a $25 war bond for singing at a spiritual contest at the Union Baptist Church. At the age of seven, Isley was already singing on stage1 at venues. Some of the venues are the Regal Theater in Chicago where he sang alongside Dinah Washington and a few other notables.

    Ronald Isley Tax Evasion

    Isley was convicted of tax evasion charges in 2006 and sentenced to three years and one month in prison. His sentence was affirmed by the United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit.

    He was imprisoned at the Federal Correctional Institution at Terre Haute, Indiana, and was scheduled for release on April 13, 2010. He was moved to a halfway house (Dismas House) in St. Louis, Missouri, following an early departure that October.

    After Isley completed his sentence, he was released from a federal halfway house on April 13, 2010. Isley is currently listed as one of California’s most delinquent taxpayers, with a $303,411.43 debt from a lien filed on October 22, 2002.
